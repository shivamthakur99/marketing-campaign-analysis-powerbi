## ğŸ” Key Analysis Performed

### 1. Demographic Insights

* A majority of customers are **married and highly educated**, though statistical testing shows no significant correlation between marital status and education.
* **Education level positively correlates with income**, indicating higher earning potential among more educated customers.
* **Household size (number of children)** shows a negative relationship with income and discretionary spending.

### 2. Income as a Key Business Driver

* Income acts as a strong proxy for multiple customer behaviors.
* Higher income is positively associated with:

  * Total spending
  * Wine and meat consumption
  * Campaign acceptance
* Income is negatively correlated with website visits, suggesting that high-income customers are more decisive buyers rather than frequent browsers.

### 3. Product-Level Insights

* Wine spending is strongly linked to:

  * Higher income
  * Meat consumption
  * Purchases through catalog and physical stores
* Wine and meat emerge as complementary premium product categories.

### 4. Campaign Performance

* Customers with higher income demonstrate a greater likelihood of accepting marketing campaigns.
* Income-based targeting proves more effective than marital-status-based segmentation.

### 5. Time-Series Trends

* From Q3â€“Q4 2012 to Q1 2013, income and spending increased simultaneously, indicating strong demand.
* By 2014, spending dropped to its lowest point despite continued income growth, highlighting a disconnect between purchasing power and consumer behavior.

## ğŸ’¡ Business Implications

* Income is a more reliable predictor of customer value than marital status.
* High-income, highly educated customers represent a premium segment with strong revenue potential.
* Customers with children have constrained discretionary spending, especially for non-essential products.
* Declining spending despite rising income signals potential disengagement or ineffective campaign strategies.

---

## ğŸš€ Recommendations

* Prioritize high-income customers for premium and personalized campaigns, especially for wine and meat products.
* Leverage catalog and in-store channels for high-value segments, emphasizing curated and exclusive experiences.
* Design value-based or bundle offers to engage families with children.
* Combine income with behavioral metrics (purchase frequency, channel preference, campaign response) for better segmentation.
* Re-evaluate campaign messaging and timing to convert income growth into actual spending.
